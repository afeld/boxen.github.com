But really, what is this "Boxen" thing?

Boxen is a framework and standard library for literally automating your very existence. Or your laptop. They're basically the same thing. We originally build it as this thing called "The Setup" for our folks here at GitHub. Then we thought to ourselves "Holy smokes, Batman — Other people would love to have this!" Okay, fine. It wasn't our idea. You heard about what we were doing and said "Sign me up!" Why? Because you hate the idea of artisanal development setups as much as we do.

What does it really do though?

Boxen automates anything and everything you tell it. Too broad? Okay. For anyone using Boxen to automate their personal setup or their organization's setup, you can use the tooling we've created to offer a one-line script to your folks they then run and in about 15 minutes they get everything they need. Sounds a little too good to be true? Nah. It's just a little sprinkling of configuration management (with Puppet) and some Ruby scripts.

What sort of stuff does it come with?

Our "default stack" comes pre-assembled for you in the boxen/our-boxen repo. That comes with: Homebrew, Git, Hub, DNSMasq with a custom .dev resolver, nginx, gcc, NVM, rbenv, automatic detection for Full Disk Encryption, several NodeJS versions, and several Ruby versions. For a lot of folks, that's a pretty good starting place for writing some code. We also provide some helpful wrappers for making configuring projects super simple.

Does it support other stuff?

Totally! We have about a bajillion (est.) Puppet "modules" (self-contained libraries) optimized for use with Boxen available under the @boxen org.

But I don't know Puppet!

That's okay. We've provided a bunch of helpful docs to get started, aimed at beginners.

What about Linux, or Windows?

Right now, it's OSX (Mountain Lion or above) only. We develop primarily on OSX, so that's our primary target. That said, Puppet is really good at supporting multiple platforms, so it's literally "just code" that needs to be written. We'll always review contributions, so if you want Boxen to run on your distribution, send us some Pull Requests!

Do you have plans for …?

Maybe? Let's get some discussion started! Drop some issues or Pull Requests on us.
