What is "Boxen?"

Boxen is your company's IT robot. It's a dangerously opinionated framework that automates every piece of your team's development environment.

No seriously, what is it?

GitHub, Inc. wrote the first version of Boxen (called "The Setup") to let employees get things done on day one, without any fuss. It's configuration management for everyone; even our HR department uses it. Boxen embodies the philosophy that "development is production." It values consistency, predictability, and reprocability over grass-fed, free-range, artisanal development environments.

Boxen is designed from the ground up to be more modular to be easily usable by any company, working on anything at all: not just GitHub. More importantly, Boxen places a priority on standardization of the ecosystem — something that configuration management has been lacking. Any Puppet module designed properly with Boxen in mind will be pluggable into your particular configuration, no matter what.

What can it do?

Boxen can clone your projects, install your toolchains, apply security policies, configure your favorite music player, set your favorite screensaver, and make your life a whole lot easier. We've built a "default stack" targeted at web developers in the boxen/our-boxen repo. The template demonstrates just a few things possible like forcing your employees to be secure by default with Full Disk Encryption or installing packages from Homebrew. The template also comes with a handful of Ruby versions, some Node versions, nginx, and a custom .dev DNS resolver.

We've already written roughly a bajillion libraries for use with Boxen that cover everything from configuring elasticsearch to installing Minecraft. Be sure to customize your company's Boxen environment as well. Anything not managed by Boxen — whether it's the dependencies for your application or your video games — is a bug.

How do I write my own libraries?

All the magic in Boxen is done with Puppet. New to Puppet? Never fear — we've written up documentation aimed at beginners and experts alike.

What about Linux, or Windows?

Right now, it's OSX (Mountain Lion or above) only. We develop primarily on OSX, so that's our primary target. That said, Puppet is really good at supporting multiple platforms, so it's literally "just code" that needs to be written. We'll always review contributions, so if you want Boxen to run on your distribution, send us some Pull Requests!
